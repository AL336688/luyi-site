Australia-based perspective

A Chinese product needs more than translation to become relevant in an English-speaking market.

Working from Australia gives me a real-world view of how people live, shop, compare quality and decide what feels worth paying for. That matters when a product is trying to enter a market with different habits, retail references and expectations.

I work across Australia, the United States and the United Kingdom, helping teams reconsider product opportunity, audience positioning, packaging value, pricing logic and market fit before they spend more on design, advertising or distribution.

My Edge

My value is not teaching you how to operate a specific platform.

Many consumer products are not poor in quality. Their value simply has not been made visible: the message is wrong, the audience is too broad, the packaging has no price signal, or an idea that works in China becomes an obstacle in an English-speaking market.

I am most useful before you spend more on samples, design, advertising or channels. I help you judge whether the direction is sound, and how the product can become more brand-led and better able to support its intended price.

What I Diagnose

I usually start by looking at these six things.

01

Product opportunity

Is this worth continuing to invest in, or is it simply a product or resource already at hand?

02

Audience focus

Who is more willing to pay, whether the use case is specific enough, and whether the need is strong enough.

03

Value proposition

Whether the current message is the language users actually care about, and whether it can become a reason to pay more.

04

Packaging & price signal

Whether the material, naming, packaging, visual direction and comparison set support the price band you want.

05

Market fit overseas

Whether people in Australia, the US, the UK and other English-speaking markets share the same usage habits and purchase logic.

06

Next priority

Whether to change the product, audience, packaging or price, or to pause and reassess the direction first.

Best Fit

Who I am best placed to help.

  • Teams with a product, sample or supply chain who want to build a real brand
  • Teams whose product quality is solid, but whose price will not hold
  • Teams moving from factory goods or white-label goods towards consumer brands
  • Teams using an overseas perspective to rethink the expression of a Chinese consumer product
  • Teams entering English-speaking markets who are not sure whether local users will buy

Boundary

What I do not primarily provide.

  • Platform-rule training, including Amazon or Shopify back-office and basic e-commerce operations
  • Advertising buying, content-platform operations or social-media account management
  • Direct packaging artwork, visual design execution or pure production design services
  • Short-term order chasing without a willingness to revisit the product and its positioning
